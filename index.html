Select anchor and click Price Option

Connects to Deribit via WebSocket. Fetches live mark_iv and DVOL index for all options. Prices using Black-76 with r = 0, exactly as Deribit does.

For non-anchor tokens, the Deribit surface shape is preserved while the DVOL level is adjusted via 30-day realized HV ratio. Inverse options show premium in coin, BTC equivalent, and USD value.

Deribit Pricing Methodology
Black-76 · r = 0

Deribit prices all options with Black-76 and r = 0. The forward is simply F = S (the index price). No interest rate carry, no borrow cost. Greeks are computed vs the forward price.

DVOL Index

Deribit's DVOL is a 30-day constant-maturity IV index calculated from live BTC/ETH option order books using a variance-swap replication methodology (similar to VIX). It sets the level of the surface.

Live Surface Shape

Surface shape (smile, skew, wings, term structure) from live Deribit mark_iv quotes fetched over WebSocket. Bilinear interpolation in log-moneyness × √T space.

Mark IV Calibration

Each option's mark_iv is Deribit's own calibrated IV. The surface is used directly — no HV scaling is applied when pricing BTC or ETH against their own anchor. DVOL anchors the ATM level.

Non-Anchor Tokens

For tokens without Deribit options (SOL, XRP, etc.), the anchor surface shape is borrowed and the IV level is shifted by target_HV / anchor_HV. Override with Manual IV for full control.

Inverse (Coin-Margined)

Inverse contracts are coin-settled: premium = USD_price ÷ F. Results shown in three currencies: coin premium (₿/Ξ), BTC equivalent, and USD value (coin × spot).

Expiry Precision

Expiry is specified as date + cut time (default 08:00 UTC for Deribit). T is computed to the second using exact millisecond timestamps, matching Deribit's own T calculation.

Fallback Layers

1. Deribit WebSocket (primary). 2. Deribit REST. 3. SVI parametric surface calibrated to DVOL. Manual IV override skips all layers.

Select an anchor and click Price Option

Choose any of Deribit's 6 live option markets. The surface shape is borrowed from their live mark_iv quotes. The vol level is scaled by your token's 30-day realized HV vs the anchor's.

Data is fetched over WebSocket (zero CORS restrictions). Three automatic fallback layers ensure the app never fails to produce a surface.

Methodology & Fallback Architecture
Layer 1 — Deribit WebSocket

Primary: connects to wss://www.deribit.com/ws/api/v2. No CORS restrictions, no auth required. Sends get_book_summary_by_currency JSON-RPC over the socket. Receives full live mark_iv for every option. Timeout: 12s.

Layer 2 — Deribit REST fallback

If WS fails: tries https://www.deribit.com/api/v2/public/... REST endpoint. Works when CORS headers are present (they usually are for public endpoints). Also tries get_historical_volatility for DVOL-derived ATM level.

Layer 3 — SVI Synthetic Surface

If both fail: builds a full parametric vol surface using SVI (Stochastic Volatility Inspired) with calibrated parameters per anchor. ATM level from Deribit DVOL if available, else hard-coded. Shape is realistic: put skew, call wing flattening, contango term structure.

HV Scaling

Fetches 32 daily closes from Binance klines (CoinGecko fallback) for anchor and target. Computes annualized 30-day log-return σ. Every IV on surface multiplied by HV_target / HV_anchor. Smile shape identical; only level shifts.

Interpolation

Bilinear in log-moneyness × √T space. Smile slices per expiry are built from nearby options. For the SVI surface, the parametric form gives clean closed-form values everywhere with no extrapolation artifacts.

Pricing

Standard European Black-Scholes-Merton. Greeks computed analytically: Δ, Γ, Θ/day, V/1%, ρ/1%. Break-even, intrinsic, and time value also shown. Notional converts to contract count.
